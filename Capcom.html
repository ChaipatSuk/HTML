5.Okami HD

Amaterasu’s celestial brush item will help her overcome Okami’s numerous challenges.With it, you can

create vine-like grappling hooks or uncover hidden secrets that are buried in Japan.Okami’s HD re-releases

improve the original game’s graphics and technical problems, making it the definitive way to experience this

remarkable gem. With its gripping storyline, mesmerizing visuals, and stellar protagonist,

you’d be foolish not to give this game a shot.


4.Monster Hunter Rise

In it, players control a monster hunter. He’ll take on numerous missions that involve him or hero retrieving

ingredients or defeating dangerous creatures. Monster Hunter Rise is the latest installment of the franchise.

It introduces monsters from the past and present. The game feels less sluggish compared to other entries

and includes better transportation aspects. Instead of having a cat-like companion, you can fight monsters

with a canine too. You can also ride atop the canine for travel purposes. Furthermore,

Monster Hunter Rise includes a fabulous wire bug game mechanic that will make you feel like Spider-Man.

That’s not all. In Monster Hunter Rise, you can ride atop the beasts you fight and use them to battle

other monsters in epic kaiju bouts. In addition to having a decent storyline, sharper graphics,

and a great soundtrack, this game will provide you with endless amounts of fun.


3.Street Fighter II: The World Warrior

The newcomers came with stages of their own. Each reflects their stand-in character’s backgrounds and

personalities well. Furthermore, Street Fighter II helped video game arcade businesses prosper

due to the title’s competitive nature. It went on to inspire countless tournament scenes

including the Evolution Championship Series (EVO). From its stellar combo system, memorable roster,

and charming visuals and soundtrack, Street Fighter II is one of Capcom’s finest games to date.


2.Devil May Cry 5

The story has you switching between each protagonist. It does its best to flesh out past story elements

that were left unchecked. The game’s graphics are impeccable from the backgrounds to each character’s

facial and bodily characteristics. The action is just as great as previous entries as you’ll have a blast

slicing your way past hellish enemies. The game’s three-character approach can carry mixed baggage from

a progression standpoint. The difficulty spikes near the game’s final moments can annoy some folks, too.

Nonetheless, Devil May Cry V is one of those games that encourage players to return to it several times

due to its sheer amount of fan service and effort put into it.


1.Resident Evil 2 (Remake)

In it, we follow college student Claire Redfield and police officer Leon Kennedy as they embark on a

treacherous journey through Raccoon City. You’ll be playing from Claire and Leon’s perspective and

navigating through a campaign centered around their characters. Many people who weren’t fans of the

original’s tank-like controls will find the remake more exciting to play The zombies react to your attacks

more authentically in the remake. Players will enjoy ramming through zombie hordes, exploring

dark corridors, and solving puzzles to gain access to new locations. With its creepy atmosphere,

compelling combat, and glorious visuals, players will be impressed by the amount of effort

that went into crafting Resident Evil 2’s remake.
